## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### 5-7 Key Hypotheses

| # | Hypothesis | Severity | Confidence | Validation Priority |
|---|-----------|----------|------------|-------------------|
| H1 | **Brand Identity Fragmentation** — Customers are confused by 3 overlapping brand names (AURA · CAFE, Aura Coffee, Container Café) leading to weak brand recall and diluted word-of-mouth | High | Medium | P0 |
| H2 | **Discovery Friction** — Sa Đéc's digital discovery ecosystem (Google Maps, Facebook, Zalo) is underutilized; customers rely on physical visibility and word-of-mouth, limiting reach beyond foot traffic | High | High | P0 |
| H3 | **Container Novelty Wears Off** — The container aesthetic drives initial visits but does not sustain repeat behavior without differentiated product/experience quality | Medium | Medium | P1 |
| H4 | **Remote Worker Underserved** — No café in Sa Đéc adequately serves the growing remote worker/freelancer segment (WiFi quality, power outlets, seating ergonomics, noise management) | Medium | Low | P1 |
| H5 | **VIP Room Value Proposition Unclear** — Private room offering exists but positioning (business meeting vs. group hangout vs. content creation space) is undefined, leading to underutilization | Medium | Low | P1 |
| H6 | **Tourist Experience Gap** — Sa Đéc tourism (Sa Đéc Flower Village, Huỳnh Thủy Lê House) generates foot traffic, but no café captures this segment with bilingual menus, Instagrammable moments, or tourist-specific offerings | Low | Medium | P2 |
| H7 | **Delivery Channel Leakage** — GrabFood/ShopeeFood presence is either non-existent or poorly optimized, losing the growing delivery-first customer segment in provincial Vietnam | High | Medium | P0 |

- **Critical (S1):** Directly impacts revenue, causes customer churn, blocks acquisition
- **High (S2):** Significantly degrades experience, reduces repeat rate, limits growth
- **Medium (S3):** Annoying but not deal-breaking, fixable with low effort
- **Low (S4):** Minor friction, nice-to-have improvement

|------|-----------|----------|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|

## 6. CULTURAL CONTEXT — SA Đéc / MEKONG DELTA COFFEE CULTURE

### 6.1 Sa Đéc Market Characteristics

| Factor | Description | UX Implication |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Population** | ~150,000 urban, ~200,000 metro area | Small market, word-of-mouth critical |
| **Income Level** | Lower-middle vs. HCMC/Hanoi | Price sensitivity, value-conscious |
| **Coffee Culture** | Traditional cà phê sữa đá, roadside quán | Blend tradition with modern experience |
| **Digital Adoption** | High smartphone penetration, Facebook/Zalo dominant | Digital-first marketing, Zalo OA essential |
| **Tourism** | Sa Đéc Flower Village, Huỳnh Thủy Lê House, eco-tourism | Tourist segment seasonal, experience-driven |
| **Social Dynamics** | Close-knit community, reputation matters | Quality consistency critical, bad news spreads fast |
| **Youth Demographics** | Growing Gen Z/Millennial population | Instagram/TikTok marketing, experience economy |

### 6.2 Mekong Delta Coffee Culture

| Tradition | Description | Modern Adaptation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Cà phê sữa đá** | Iced coffee with condensed milk — the default | Must be excellent quality, signature offering |
| **Quán cóc culture** | Street-side, plastic stools, social | Modernize comfort while keeping social vibe |
| **Morning ritual** | Coffee as morning routine, not just beverage | Capture morning crowd with quick service |
| **Social gathering** | Café as meeting place, not just drink | Design for groups, conversations, lingering |
| **Price expectation** | 10K-25K for street coffee | Premium positioning must justify 2-3x price |

### 6.3 Cultural UX Considerations

| Consideration | Detail | Design Implication |
|---------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Face-saving** | Vietnamese culture values dignity, avoiding embarrassment | Staff training on handling complaints gracefully |
| **Group dynamics** | Social visits often in groups (2-6 people) | Seating arrangements for groups, group menus |
| **Family-friendly** | Multi-generational visits common | Family seating, kid-friendly options |
| **Gift culture** | Bringing gifts/treating others is common | Gift cards, treat-a-friend features |
| **Digital trust** | Reviews and social proof heavily influence decisions | Encourage reviews, showcase social proof |
| **Seasonal rhythms** | Flower season (Tết), flood season affect traffic | Seasonal menus, event programming aligned with calendar |

## APPENDIX A: INTERVIEW GUIDE TEMPLATE

### Opening (2 minutes)
- "Thank you for your time. We're improving the café experience in Sa Đéc and would love your honest feedback."
- "There are no right or wrong answers — we want to hear your real experience."

### Discovery (5 minutes)
1. "How did you first hear about this café?"
2. "What made you decide to visit?"
3. "Did you look up any information before coming? What did you find?"

### Experience (10 minutes)
4. "Walk me through your visit today — from arriving to ordering to sitting down."
5. "What was the best part of your experience?"
6. "What was the most frustrating part?"
7. "How does this compare to other cafés you visit?"

### Brand (3 minutes)
8. "What would you call this café if telling a friend?"
9. "Have you seen this café online? Where?"
10. "What comes to mind when you think of this café?"

### Loyalty (5 minutes)
11. "What would make you come back?"
12. "What would make you bring friends?"
13. "Is there anything that would stop you from returning?"

### Closing (2 minutes)
14. "If you could change one thing about this café, what would it be?"
15. "Is there anything else you'd like to share?"
